About This Report

Across many civilizations, the precise mathematical state of the heavens at the moment of birth was viewed as a cosmic signature. By mapping the intersections of astronomical coordinates with traditional zodiac systems, ancient sciences sought to outline the baseline patterns of individual psychology, temperament, and life potentials.

This report reconstructs your personal planetary blueprint, focusing on your rising sign (Lagna), key planetary influences, house configurations, and lunar mansions (Nakshatras). It maps out the relational dynamics between planets and houses, clarifying your primary psychological axes and behavioral indicators.

Individuals often study their Life Map to gain objective clarity on core behavioral traits, natural inclinations, and recurring themes. It serves as a tool for self-understanding, showing how different planetary forces may play out in your personal development and relationship dynamics.

Your Rising Sign

Tula / Libra Ascendant (Lagna Profile)
Discover the cosmic mask you wear, your vital energy, and how you naturally navigate the outer world.

Tula (Libra) is governed by Shukra (Venus), the planet of beauty, harmony, aesthetics, and relational intelligence. The Libra ascendant imprints the native with a natural orientation toward balance, diplomacy, and the cultivation of partnerships. The physical presence tends toward grace and composure, and the native characteristically approaches life's challenges through negotiation, aesthetic refinement, and the careful weighing of all available perspectives before committing to a position.

As a cardinal air sign, Tula rising generates an intellect that is quick to assess situations from multiple angles. This quality can appear as indecisiveness to observers, but it is in fact a deeply systematic form of judgment — one that weighs all variables before acting. The Lagna lord Venus occupies the 7th house in Aries, placing the native's life force squarely within the domain of relationships, public alliances, and one-on-one dynamics. This is a chart that finds its meaning through others.

The classical Vedic aphorism Lagnesh in Saptam Bhava — the ruler of the 1st in the 7th — points to a life path in which partnership, alliance, and collaboration are not peripheral but central. The self is most fully expressed when engaged with another. Professional and personal success tend to arrive through co-creative effort rather than solitary endeavour.

Key Chart Patterns

Notable Chart Configurations
Explore the unique groupings of planetary forces that define the structural themes of your destiny.
① 8th House Stellium — Sun, Mercury ℞ & Jupiter in Vrishabha

Three major planets converging in the 8th house is an uncommon and high-signal configuration. The 8th is the domain of transformation, hidden knowledge, research, inheritance, and the threshold between the visible and invisible worlds. Sun (soul direction), Mercury retrograde (deeply inward-turning intelligence), and Jupiter (wisdom, expansion) all operating through Taurus — the sign of patient persistence, material depth, and accumulated knowledge — create a personality strongly drawn to research, esoteric study, advisory roles, and the careful stewardship of knowledge or resources. Mercury retrograde in this placement suggests a mind that processes information by going deeply inward before surfacing with refined conclusions.

② Rahu in 10th / Ketu in 4th — The Career-Home Karmic Axis

Rahu in Cancer in the 10th house places the soul's karmic hunger squarely in the domain of public recognition, authority, and career achievement. Cancer as the sign introduces an emotionally nuanced approach to professional life — success arrives through nurturing leadership, and industries connected to care, community, or public welfare. Ketu in Capricorn in the 4th house indicates a soul actively releasing attachment to fixed domestic structures, ancestral expectations, and conventional security in this lifetime. The deep home-based certainties of past cycles are loosened so that public life and outward contribution may take precedence.

③ Venus in 7th — Lagna Lord in the House of Partnerships

Venus rules Libra (the Lagna) and occupies the 7th house in Aries. The Lagna lord in the 7th creates a life orientation in which personal identity is shaped, tested, and ultimately refined through one-on-one relationships — romantic, professional, and collaborative. Partnerships are not simply a chapter in this life; they are the entire structure through which the self is understood. The Aries energy in the 7th suggests that partners of significance tend to be assertive, independent, and action-oriented, acting as catalysts for the native's own growth and direction.

Key Planetary Combinations

Planetary Yogas
Reveal the special celestial alignments in your birth chart that activate extraordinary potential and blessings.

Yogas are specific planetary combinations that modify the chart's overall signature. Classical Jyotish identifies hundreds of Yogas; the following three are active and significant in this nativity.

I
Lagnesh in Saptam — Rajayoga Tendency
Venus (Lagna Lord) in 7th House

When the ruler of the 1st house (the Lagna lord) occupies the 7th house, a classical Rajayoga tendency is formed. The self and the domain of partnerships become deeply intertwined — personal power, social recognition, and life advancement arrive through alliances rather than solitary effort. For this chart, Venus as the Lagna lord in the 7th amplifies the natural Libra orientation toward collaboration, making partnership-driven endeavours the primary engine of growth and achievement.

II
Viparita Raja Yoga
6th Lord (Jupiter) + 12th Lord (Mercury) both in 8th House

Viparita Raja Yoga — "reversed royal combination" — forms when lords of dusthana (difficult) houses (6th, 8th, 12th) occupy other dusthana houses. For Libra Lagna, Jupiter rules the 6th (Pisces) and Mercury rules the 12th (Virgo). Both are placed in the 8th house. This creates a paradoxical strength: challenges, obstacles, and hidden pressures ultimately convert into sources of power, resilience, and unexpected breakthrough. The native tends to rise precisely through what others consider adversity.

III
Trikona Moon — Lakshmi Yoga Tendency
Moon in 9th House (Trikona)

A benefic planet in a Trikona (1st, 5th, or 9th house) creates auspicious conditions for that planet's natural qualities to flourish. Moon in the 9th house — the house of Dharma, higher learning, and spiritual merit — produces a Lakshmi Yoga tendency: emotional fulfillment, natural philosophical grace, and an instinctive alignment with right action. The native finds genuine happiness through teaching, learning, and the pursuit of meaning rather than material accumulation alone.

1. The Eastern Horizon (Lagna)

The structural anchor of the entire matrix is the Lagna, which represents the exact degree of the zodiac sign rising on the local eastern horizon at the precise time and geographic coordinates of birth. The rising sign occupies the 1st House, acting as the gateway through which the soul enters the physical world. Once the horizon sign is locked, the remaining 11 houses are mapped sequentially in a counter-clockwise direction. Because the Earth rotates, the horizon shifts signs roughly every two hours, making exact birth times critical to establishing the accurate grid structure.

2. The Natal Chart Matrix (The Geometric Houses)

Unlike Western circular charts, a standard North Indian Vedic natal chart uses a diamond-and-triangle grid structure containing 12 static zones (houses). Houses 1, 4, 7, and 10 form the center diamonds known as the Kendras (Quadrants). These represent the fundamental pillars of life: self, home, partner, and career. Houses 1, 5, and 9 form the Trikonas (Trines), representing the houses of purpose, luck, and past-life merits (Purva Punya). The houses are divided into four primary life pursuits (Purusharthas): Dharma (1, 5, 9), Artha (2, 6, 10), Kama (3, 7, 11), and Moksha (4, 8, 12).

3. The Lunar Mansion Matrix (Nakshatras)

While the houses represent external life spaces, the 27 Nakshatras (Lunar Mansions) form a deeper, hidden matrix of psychology and consciousness. Every planet and house cusp is embedded within a specific Nakshatra, revealing hidden traits, subconscious blocks, and inherited soul talents.
