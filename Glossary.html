Glossary of Terms

Every significant word in the Antara reports explained in plain, straightforward language for absolute computational and design clarity.

ABCD EFGH IJKL MNPR STVW YZ
A
Antara (अन्तरा)
Sanskrit · India
A Sanskrit word meaning "within" or "interior" — the inner space of a person. In music, the antarā is the second section of a composition, often the deepest and most expressive.
Think of it as your inner world — everything that makes you uniquely you, on the inside.
Archetype
Psychological & Design Theory
A recurring pattern of behavior, symbolic character type, or structural energy configuration that exists across different human cultures and time periods.
A common pattern or character type, like the explorer, builder, teacher, or protector.
Ascendant (Rising Sign)
Western & Vedic Astrology
The zodiac sign that was rising on the eastern horizon at the exact moment of your birth. In a birth chart, it marks the beginning of the First House and is considered one of the three most important points in a chart (along with Sun and Moon).
Your Ascendant is how you appear to people who meet you for the first time — your outer personality, your physical style, the "mask" you naturally wear in the world.
Ashtakavarga
Vedic Astrology · India
A comprehensive mathematical point-distribution system in Vedic astrology that evaluates the relative strength, influence, and support of planets across various sectors of a birth chart.
A Vedic astrology scoring method that gives points to areas of life to show where support is strongest and where pressure rises.
Aspects
Western Astrology · Greek
Angular relationships between planets in a birth chart. When two planets are a certain number of degrees apart (0°, 60°, 90°, 120°, 180°), they are said to form an aspect. Each angle type has a traditional meaning.
A measured angle between planets in astrology, used to describe how their meanings interact or talk to each other in your personality.
Ayanamsha
Sanskrit · Vedic Astrology
A mathematical correction applied in Vedic (sidereal) astrology to account for the slow wobble of the Earth on its axis — a phenomenon called the Precession of the Equinoxes. Antara uses the Lahiri Ayanamsha, which is the official standard in India.
Imagine you are measuring someone's position on a moving train. The Ayanamsha is the correction that accounts for the train's movement — it makes the calculation accurate to where the stars actually are, not where they appear from Earth using the simpler Western method.
B
BaZi (八字)
Chinese · Han Dynasty onwards
Literally "eight characters" in Chinese. A system of personal analysis using the Chinese sexagenary (60-cycle) calendar to assign four pairs of symbols — called Pillars — to a person's Year, Month, Day, and Hour of birth. Each pillar contains one Heavenly Stem and one Earthly Branch.
A Chinese birth-chart system based on year, month, day, and hour, breaking your nature into a recipe of elemental strengths.
Bindu
Sanskrit · Vedic Astrology
A dot, point, or coordinate marker. In Ashtakavarga, bindus represent positive energy points or beneficial scores accumulated within a specific house or planetary sector.
A point or score in some Vedic astrology systems, representing the level of supportive energy in a specific area of your chart.
Biorhythm
Modern Science · 19th–20th Century
A theory that human biological functions follow regular cycles beginning at birth. Three main cycles are widely studied: Physical (23 days), Emotional (28 days), and Intellectual (33 days). A fourth — Spiritual (53 days) — is used in Antara as well.
A repeating cycle used here to reflect physical, emotional, or intellectual energy wave patterns that rise and fall starting from the moment you were born.
C
Chaldean Numerology
Ancient Babylon · c. 2000 BCE
One of the oldest numerological systems, developed by the Chaldean priests of ancient Mesopotamia (modern-day Iraq). Unlike Pythagorean numerology, which assigns letters numbers 1–9 in alphabetical sequence, the Chaldean system assigns numbers based on the vibrational sound-frequency of each letter.
The Chaldeans believed every sound carries a vibration, and every vibration has a number. So your name is not just letters — it is a chord of vibrations that describe your energetic signature.
Cinnabar
Design Token & Alchemy
A naturally occurring bright red mineral pigment historically associated with ancient alchemical seals, traditional manuscripts, and high-status ceremonial borders.
A deep red-orange color used as a strong, striking accent in the Antara design canvas to represent high-importance nodes and thresholds.
Composite Chart
Modern Western Astrology
A chart created by taking the midpoint between corresponding planets in two people's birth charts. The result is a single "relationship chart" that describes the nature of the relationship itself, independent of either person.
A relationship chart that combines two people's birth data into one shared pattern, exploring the identity of the connection itself.
D
Dasha
Sanskrit · Vedic Astrology
A system of planetary time periods in Vedic astrology. Each planet rules a specific period of years in a person's life (the most common system gives the Moon 10 years, Mars 7, Rahu 18, etc.), and these periods cycle through in a fixed order. The Dasha period you are currently in influences the overall themes of that time.
Think of Dasha periods like chapters in a book. Each chapter has a different planet as the main character, and that planet's qualities colour everything that happens during those years.
Day Master
Chinese · BaZi System
The Heavenly Stem of the Day Pillar in a BaZi chart. It is the central reference coordinate of the entire system, representing the individual's core self, basic element, and essential identity.
In BaZi, the element that represents the person at the center of the chart — the absolute foundation for all other elemental comparisons.
E
Earthly Branches
Chinese Calendar System
Twelve symbols in the Chinese calendar system, associated with the twelve animals of the Chinese zodiac (Rat, Ox, Tiger, Rabbit, Dragon, Snake, Horse, Goat, Monkey, Rooster, Dog, Pig). They form the lower half of each BaZi Pillar.
The Earthly Branches form a 12-year cycle. Each branch rules a specific year, month, or double-hour, carrying a particular elemental energy and structural quality.
Element
Universal Symbolic Systems
A primary energetic phase, material archetype, or structural building block (such as Fire, Water, Earth, Metal, or Wood) used by ancient systems to map seasonal, mental, and physical shifts.
A symbolic building block like Fire, Water, Earth, Metal, or Wood that represents the core qualities of your personal makeup.
Ephemeris
Greek — "ephēmeros" meaning daily
A table or database recording the positions of astronomical objects (Sun, Moon, planets) at regular intervals. Antara uses ephemeris data to calculate exactly where each planet was at the time of your birth.
An Ephemeris is like a GPS history log for the planets — it records exactly where Mars, Venus, Jupiter, etc. were on every day of every year throughout history.
F
Friction Zone
Systemic Synthesis
A sector or aspect configuration where contrasting elements or opposing numerical vibrations intersect, representing a developmental tension, obstacle, or necessary growth threshold.
An area in your personal pattern or relationship chart that may need more patience, conscious awareness, or consistent effort.
G
Gematria
Hebrew Mysticism · Kabbalah
A method of assigning numerical values to letters, words, or phrases in the Hebrew alphabet. Used in Kabbalistic tradition to find hidden meanings and relationships between concepts by comparing their numerical equivalents.
In Gematria, every Hebrew letter has a number. So every word becomes a number. And if two different words share the same number, they are considered to be spiritually connected. Antara uses this system applied to your name.
H
Heavenly Stems
Chinese Calendar System
Ten symbols in the Chinese calendar system that represent the five elements (Wood, Fire, Earth, Metal, Water) in their Yin and Yang forms. They form the upper half of each BaZi Pillar.
The Heavenly Stems describe the five elements in active (Yang) and receptive (Yin) expressions, clarifying the balance of forces in your chart.
Hellenistic
Ancient Greek Astrology
The original system of horoscope-based astrology that flourished in the Mediterranean basin (especially Egypt and Greece) from the 2nd century BCE to the 6th century CE, which introduced the system of 12 Houses.
Connected to an older Greek style of astrology that focuses on geometric house alignments, angular houses, and clean astronomical divisions.
House (Astrological)
Western & Vedic Astrology
The birth chart is divided into 12 sections called Houses. Each House governs a specific area of life: the First House relates to self and appearance, the Second to money and possessions, the Seventh to relationships and partnerships, and so on. The position of planets in different Houses shows which life areas they influence.
One of 12 life areas in an astrology chart, such as work, home, money, self-expression, or relationships.
I
Interpretive (not Predictive)
Antara Brand Principle
Antara's core philosophical position. The reports interpret (describe, map, explain) what the calculations show — they do not predict (claim to know in advance) what events will happen in your life.
Meant to help you understand personal meaning and timing structures, not to prove a fixed future fact or predict specific events.
J
Jyotish
Sanskrit · India · c. 3000 BCE
The Sanskrit name for Vedic astrology. Literally translated as "science of light" (Jyoti = light, Isha = lord/science). One of the six Vedangas — the auxiliary disciplines of the Vedas, India's most ancient sacred texts. Jyotish calculates planetary positions using the sidereal zodiac (based on the actual positions of stars).
Jyotish is India's ancient system of reading the sky. It is one of the most mathematically precise astrological traditions in the world — developed over thousands of years by scholars who were also mathematicians and astronomers.
K
Kabbalah
Hebrew Mysticism · Medieval Period
A tradition of Jewish mystical thought and practice. The word comes from the Hebrew root meaning "to receive." Kabbalah includes a complex set of symbolic frameworks — including the Tree of Life (a diagram of ten divine attributes) and Gematria (letter-number correspondences).
Kabbalah is a system of Jewish wisdom that uses the Hebrew alphabet as a map of reality. Every letter is also a number and a symbol — so your name, when read through Kabbalah, becomes a kind of spiritual blueprint.
Koota
Vedic Astrology · India
An ancient point-matching metric used in Indian relationship systems (such as Ashta-Koota, worth 36 points) to evaluate structural and emotional compatibility across eight distinct behavioral layers.
A compatibility scoring idea from Indian astrology traditions used to evaluate how two people's lunar elements and charts harmonize.
Kundali
Sanskrit · Vedic Astrology
The Hindi/Sanskrit word for a birth chart in Vedic astrology. Literally means "coiled" — referring to the circular or diamond-shaped layout of the chart. A Kundali records the positions of the Sun, Moon, and all planets in the 12 houses at the moment of birth.
A Kundali is your cosmic photograph at the moment you were born — a snapshot of where every planet was in the sky, translated into a chart that astrologers can read.
L
Lahiri Ayanamsha
Named after N.C. Lahiri · Indian Government Standard
The most widely used Ayanamsha (correction factor) in Vedic astrology in India. It was officially adopted by the Indian government's Calendar Reform Committee in 1955. Named after the astronomer N.C. Lahiri who calculated it. Antara uses this standard throughout all Vedic calculations.
There are several ways to make the correction that Vedic astrology needs for Earth's wobble — Lahiri is the officially recognised Indian standard, like a certified measuring stick.
Lagna
Sanskrit · Vedic Astrology
The Sanskrit term for the Ascendant in Vedic astrology — the zodiac sign rising on the eastern horizon at the time of birth. The Lagna marks the First House of the birth chart and is considered the most personally significant point.
The rising sign or starting point of a Vedic birth chart, defining your fundamental physical identity and outer orientation.
Life Path Number
Pythagorean Numerology
The most important number in Pythagorean numerology. Calculated by adding together all the digits in your full birth date and reducing them to a single digit (1–9), or keeping 11, 22, 33 as "master numbers." It describes the main theme and direction of your life.
Your Life Path is like the genre of your life story — it does not tell you the plot, but it describes the kind of journey you are on. A Life Path 1 is a story about leadership and independence; a Life Path 7 is a story about wisdom and inner depth.
Lo Shu Grid (洛書)
Ancient China · c. 2800 BCE
A 3×3 magic square in which every row, column, and diagonal adds up to 15. According to Chinese legend, it was discovered on the back of a tortoise that emerged from the Lo River during the reign of Emperor Yu. In numerology, the digits of your birth date are placed into the grid to reveal patterns of strength, balance, and absence.
A 3x3 number grid used in Chinese numerology and symbolic analysis to map out personality strengths, habits, and developmental gaps.
Luck Cycles (大運 · Dà Yùn)
Chinese · BaZi System
Ten-year periods in BaZi analysis during which a person's elemental environment shifts. Each cycle is represented by a different Heavenly Stem and Earthly Branch combination, influencing which types of experiences and opportunities arise during that decade.
Imagine your life as a long journey through different climate zones. Each Luck Cycle is like spending a decade in a different climate — some years you are in a warm, supportive environment; in others, the conditions are more challenging. Knowing the cycle helps you dress for the weather.
M
Master Numbers
Pythagorean Numerology
In Pythagorean numerology, the numbers 11, 22, and 33 are called Master Numbers. Unlike other two-digit results, these are not reduced to a single digit. They carry amplified energy compared to their single-digit equivalents (2, 4, and 6 respectively).
Most numbers in numerology get reduced to 1–9 by adding their digits together. But 11, 22, and 33 are considered special — they carry extra intensity. If your Life Path is 11, you have the qualities of a 2 (sensitivity, cooperation) but turned up much higher.
N
Nakshatra
Sanskrit · Vedic Astrology
One of the 27 lunar star mansions or constellations through which the moon travels. Each Nakshatra spans 13°20' of the zodiac and governs specific phonetic seed syllables, ruling elements, and behavioral attributes.
One of 27 lunar star sections used in Vedic astrology, representing the Moon's structural alignment at birth.
Navamsha
Sanskrit · Vedic Astrology
A divisional chart in Vedic astrology, created by dividing each zodiac sign into nine equal parts. It is considered the most important divisional chart and gives deeper insight into the soul's purpose, relationships (especially marriage), and the second half of life.
Think of the Navamsha as a magnifying glass placed over your birth chart — it reveals finer details that the main chart does not show, especially about your deeper motivations and your relationships.
Numerology
Universal Symbolic Systems
A collection of mathematical, symbolic systems (including Pythagorean, Chaldean, Tamil Vedic, and Kabbalah) that study the qualitative relationships between numeric sequences, birth timelines, and alphabet character coordinates.
A symbolic system that studies numbers connected to names, letters, and dates to identify underlying patterns.
P
Pillars (Four Pillars)
Chinese · BaZi System
The four columns in a BaZi chart — Year Pillar, Month Pillar, Day Pillar, and Hour Pillar. Each pillar is derived from converting your birth date and time into the Chinese sexagenary calendar. Together they form your "eight characters" (BaZi).
In BaZi, one pair of symbols ( Heavenly Stem and Earthly Branch) connected with your year, month, day, or hour of birth.
Precession of the Equinoxes
Astronomy · Observed since antiquity
The slow wobble of the Earth on its axis, which causes the position of the equinoxes to drift slowly through the zodiac over a cycle of approximately 26,000 years. This is the reason Vedic astrology (which tracks actual star positions) differs from Western astrology (which uses a fixed reference point set in antiquity).
Imagine a spinning top that slowly wobbles in a big circle as it spins. Earth does the same thing over 26,000 years. This means "where the Sun is" at any given moment gradually shifts relative to the actual stars — which is why Vedic and Western astrology end up giving different zodiac signs for the same birthday.
Progression
Western Astrology
An advanced symbolic timing technique in Western astrology that maps a person's inner growth and timing by equating "one day in the ephemeris after birth" to "one year of lived life."
An astrology technique used to track slow, long-term personal growth and inner developmental changes over time.
Pythagorean Numerology
Ancient Greece · Pythagoras · c. 570 BCE
The most widely known Western numerological system, attributed to the Greek mathematician and philosopher Pythagoras of Samos. It assigns the numbers 1–9 to the letters of the alphabet in sequence (A=1, B=2, … I=9, J=1, K=2, etc.) and reduces names and dates to single-digit numbers through addition.
Pythagoras — the same person who gave us the famous triangle theorem — believed that numbers were the hidden language of the universe. His numerology assigns a number to each letter of your name and reduces them to find your core numeric identity.
R
Rasi Chakra
Sanskrit · Vedic Astrology · South Indian Style
The South Indian style of drawing a Vedic birth chart. "Rasi" means "zodiac sign" and "Chakra" means "wheel" or "grid." Unlike the North Indian diamond format, the South Indian chart places the zodiac signs in fixed positions in a square grid, and marks the Ascendant within whichever sign it falls in.
It is the same information as a Kundali (North Indian chart) — but drawn as a square grid rather than a diamond. Different regions of India prefer different visual styles, just as some people prefer pie charts and others prefer bar graphs.
Remedy
Symbolic & Behavioral Balance
A practical recommendation, behavioral adjustments, physical environment upgrades, or habit configurations designed to neutralize energetic pressure or balance missing elements in a birth map.
A suggested practice, habit, workspace layout, color, object, or action meant to bring balance to an elemental pattern or developmental gap.
Resonance
Acoustics & Symbolic Alignment
The reinforcement or reinforcement-like amplification of a quality, frequency, or coordinate when it intersects a matching coordinate in another symbolic system (e.g. name coordinate matching birth coordinate).
A feeling of matching, echoing, or working well together—like two notes that create a pleasing, stable harmony when played at once.
S
Saffron
Design Token & Heritage
A highly valued deep golden-orange pigment historically associated with spiritual focus, mental clarity, and energetic emphasis.
A warm orange color used for focus, action, and strategic visual emphasis across the Antara checkout and visual interfaces.
Sidereal Zodiac
Astronomy · Used in Vedic Astrology
A system of measuring the zodiac that is based on the actual positions of the fixed stars. "Sidereal" comes from the Latin "sidus" meaning "star." Vedic astrology uses the sidereal zodiac, which means the sign positions it calculates correspond to where the constellations actually are in the sky today.
The sidereal zodiac tracks the real stars. If Vedic astrology says Jupiter is in Aries, Jupiter is actually in the region of sky where the Aries stars are. This is different from the Western tropical zodiac, which uses a reference point fixed 2,000 years ago.
Synastry
Western Astrology · Greek "syn" (together) + "astron" (star)
The comparison of two birth charts by overlaying them. Astrologers look at how one person's planets fall in relation to the other person's houses and planets. Strong connections (conjunctions, trines) between the two charts indicate areas of natural harmony; challenging connections (squares, oppositions) indicate productive friction or areas requiring work.
A relationship astrology method comparing two separate people's birth charts to look at their natural conversational points and compatibility.
T
Tamil Vedic Numerology
South India · Dravidian Tradition
A numerological system from the Tamil Siddha tradition of South India, which connects birth numbers directly to the nine planets of Vedic astrology. Each number from 1 to 9 is ruled by a specific planet, and that planet's qualities influence the character of anyone born under that number.
In Tamil numerology, your birth date number is not just a number — it is a planet. Born on the 3rd? Your number is 3, ruled by Jupiter. Everything Jupiter stands for (wisdom, expansion, teaching) influences your nature. It is numerology married to planetary astrology.
Ten Gods
Chinese · BaZi System
In BaZi, the "Ten Gods" (or Ten Deities) are ten relationship archetypes that describe how each element in your chart relates to your Day Stem (your core self). They have evocative names like "Direct Wealth," "Indirect Wealth," "Hurting Officer," "Rob Wealth," etc., and describe different life dynamics.
The Ten Gods are like ten different roles that each planet can play in your life story — one might be the supportive mentor, another the competitive rival, another the creative inspiration. They describe the drama around your core self.
Transit
Astrology · Western & Vedic
The current position of a planet in the sky, as it moves through different zodiac signs and houses over time. When a transiting planet crosses over the position of a natal (birth) planet, it creates a temporary activation of that natal planet's themes in a person's life.
The current movement of a planet in the sky compared with your birth chart, representing short-term or seasonal changes.
Tropical Zodiac
Western Astrology · Greek / Roman
A system of measuring the zodiac that is based on the seasons — specifically the relationship between Earth and the Sun — rather than the actual positions of the stars. The Vernal (Spring) Equinox is always defined as the beginning of Aries, regardless of where the actual Aries constellation is in the sky.
The tropical zodiac is tied to the seasons: spring always starts in Aries, summer in Cancer, autumn in Libra, winter in Capricorn. It describes your relationship to Earth's annual cycle around the Sun, not the actual star positions above you.
V
Vedanga
Sanskrit · Ancient India
One of the six auxiliary disciplines of the Vedas — the most ancient scriptures of Indian civilisation. The six Vedangas are: Shiksha (phonetics), Chandas (metre), Vyakarana (grammar), Nirukta (etymology), Kalpa (ritual), and Jyotish (astronomy/astrology). Jyotish — astrology — is one of these six.
Think of the Vedas as the main textbook and the Vedangas as the six subject guides that help you understand it. Jyotish is one of these guides — developed to track time and celestial cycles as needed for Vedic rituals.
Vedic
Sanskrit · Ancient India
Connected to the historical Indian literature, philosophy, astronomy, and mathematical disciplines originating from the ancient Vedic period (c. 1500–500 BCE).
Connected to ancient Indian astrological, astronomical, and philosophical traditions.
Vellum
Design Token & Heritage
A fine parchment historically prepared from animal skin, widely appreciated in manuscript design for its warm, slightly textured, and translucent ivory-yellow surface.
In design terminology, this represents a warm paper background rather than a stark, bright white screen.
Vibration (Numerological)
Numerology — General
In numerological traditions (especially Chaldean and Tamil Vedic), every letter, number, and sound is believed to carry a specific vibrational frequency — similar to how musical notes vibrate at different frequencies. The "vibration" of a name or number describes its energetic quality.
Just as a low guitar string vibrates differently from a high one — and each has a different sound and feel — every number is believed to carry a different kind of energy or feeling. Your name's vibration is the sum of all its letter-energies.
W
Wu Xing (Five Elements)
Chinese Philosophy
The five elemental phases in Chinese philosophy: Wood (木), Fire (火), Earth (土), Metal (金), and Water (水). They describe the cyclical transformation of energy — each element generates the next (Wood feeds Fire, Fire creates Earth/ash, Earth contains Metal, Metal collects Water, Water nourishes Wood) and can also be controlled by others.
The Five Elements are not literal materials but five patterns of energy. Wood is growth and expansion. Fire is transformation and passion. Earth is stability and nourishment. Metal is precision and contraction. Water is fluidity and wisdom. Your BaZi chart shows your elemental balance — how much of each you were born with.
Y
Yin and Yang
Chinese Philosophy
The two complementary and opposing forces in Chinese philosophy. Yin is associated with receptivity, darkness, rest, and the feminine principle. Yang is associated with activity, light, expansion, and the masculine principle. Every element in the BaZi system is classified as either Yin or Yang.
Yin and Yang are like the two sides of a coin — inseparable, each defining the other. Night defines day. Rest defines activity. In BaZi, your personal Yin-Yang balance describes whether your natural style tends toward active assertion or quiet reception.
Yong Shen (用神)
Chinese · BaZi System
Literally translated as "Useful God" or "Balancing Element." The most crucial element in a BaZi chart needed to restore balance, support the Day Master, and maximize overall life flow.
In BaZi, the useful, helpful, or balancing element for a person's chart, indicating which environment or focus brings them the most support.
Z
Zodiac
Greek — "zōidiakos kyklos" meaning circle of animals
The band of sky through which the Sun, Moon, and planets appear to move as seen from Earth. It is divided into 12 equal sections of 30° each, named after the constellations that once occupied those sections. Western astrology uses a fixed version (tropical) anchored to the seasons; Vedic astrology uses a star-based version (sidereal).
The 12-sign circle used in astrology (Aries, Taurus, Gemini, and so on), dividing the sun's annual path into twelve symbolic sectors.
