About This Report

Natural cycles govern the physical, emotional, and cognitive pacing of human life. From ancient observations of seasonal and lunar rhythms to modern mathematical models of biological wave patterns, systems have sought to track these recurring high- and low-tide coordinates to better manage vital energy.

This report tracks your concentric personal biorhythms—physical, emotional, and intellectual—against specific calendar target coordinates. It evaluates wave intersections, energy crossings, and cycle progress bars to forecast active phases and potential periods of internal friction or restoration.

Users utilize this scan to align their daily activities, important projects, and rest periods with their natural cycle momentum. It functions as an interpretive compass for emotional awareness, cognitive pacing, and physical energy management, rather than a predictive forecast.

    The Three Classical Cycles

    Life moves in natural waves. First articulated in the late 1800s and refined in the 1920s, biorhythm theory tracks three independent internal clocks that begin at birth and continue uninterrupted through life. This report maps these classical forces: The Physical Clock (23 days) anchors your body's stamina, endurance, and immune resilience; The Emotional Clock (28 days) governs your inner mood baseline, sensitivity, and relational warmth; while The Intellectual Clock (33 days) shapes your memory retrieval, analytical speed, and overall capacity for complex problem-solving.

    Reading the High, Low, and Midline Tides

    Each wave oscillates smoothly between plus one and minus one. Days near +100% represent your high peak phase, where actions feel supported, ideas materialize quickly, and fatigue recedes. Days near -100% indicate a natural low valley—this is not a failure, but a structured rest cycle where your system retains resources. Days crossing 0% are Critical Days; as a cycle changes direction, your internal system experiences a brief shift in gears. Treat these transition boundaries with simple, gentle care rather than avoidance.
