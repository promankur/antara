The Name
Antara अन्तरा

In Sanskrit, antara refers to the interior —
the inner space, the inward passage,
the depth beneath the visible surface.

In Indian classical music, the antara is the second movement of a composition:
the passage that leaves the opening refrain
and moves inward toward emotional and melodic depth.

The distinction from antar (अन्तर — gap, difference) is intentional.
Where antar implies absence between two points,
Antara implies inner substance — depth, not distance.

The name was not invented. It was recognised.

Frequently asked

The questions

Are you predicting my future?
No. Antara calculates numbers; it does not interpret them. A thermometer measures temperature — it does not tell you what to wear. Interpretive, not Predictive appears on every report as philosophy, not disclaimer.
Do you believe in astrology or numerology?
The creator is a rationalist. Belief is not required for calculation. Whether the traditions are true is a question Antara does not answer. Whether the numbers are correct is one it insists on.
Is any of this scientific?
The calculations are mathematically precise. Whether the underlying traditions have predictive scientific validity is a separate question — one Antara does not take a position on. These are internally consistent systems refined over centuries by some of the sharpest mathematical minds of their eras.
Why nine systems? Why not just one?
Because traditions disagree — and that disagreement is itself informative. Where Vedic, Western, BaZi, and numerology all point to the same characteristic, the convergence is notable. Antara's synthesis report, Chatur-Drishti, is built specifically to find those convergence points.
Who is Antara for?
For the curious. For the person who wants to know what their astrologer is actually working from. For the rationalist who wants data without doctrine. For the believer who deserves better data than they have been getting. Antara does not require belief — it only requires a birth date.
What do I do with the reports?
Whatever you like. Take them to your pandit or practitioner. Read them and reflect. Frame the cover. Share them with someone whose birth chart has always intrigued you. The reports are typeset documents produced to a standard worth keeping.
What does Antara mean?
Antara (अन्तरा) refers to the interior — the depth beneath the visible surface. In Indian classical music, the antara is the movement toward emotional depth. The deviation from antar (अन्तर — gap, difference) is deliberate: depth, not distance. The name was not invented. It was recognised.
