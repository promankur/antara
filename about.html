The Name
Antara अन्तरा

In Sanskrit, antara refers to the interior —
the inner space, the inward passage, the depth beneath the visible surface.

In Indian classical music, the antara is the second movement of a composition:
the passage that leaves the opening refrain and moves inward toward emotional and melodic depth.

The name was chosen because these traditions, regardless of their claims,
are ultimately concerned with interior patterns —
identity, temperament, rhythm, timing, memory, and meaning.

Not prediction. Interiority.

The distinction from अन्तर (antar — gap, separation, difference) is intentional.
Where antar implies absence between two points,
Antara implies inner substance — depth, not distance.

The name was not invented. It was recognised.
